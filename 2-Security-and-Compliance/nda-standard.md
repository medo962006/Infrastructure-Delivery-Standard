# 🤝 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) - Standard Template

Protecting your intellectual property, business logic, and infrastructure architecture is a fundamental requirement of our professional relationship. 

I am fully prepared to sign your company’s standard NDA. However, if you do not have one prepared, I provide this **Standard Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement** as a starting point to ensure both parties are protected before we begin exchanging technical details, credentials, or sensitive business information.

---

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" includes all non-public, proprietary information shared during our engagement, including but not limited to:
*   Application source code and architecture.
*   Cloud infrastructure credentials (IAM keys, VPN configs, SSH keys).
*   Database structures, customer data, and API keys.
*   Business strategies, roadmaps, and project documentation.

## 2. Obligations of the Recipient
*   **Non-Disclosure:** I agree to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose it to any third party.
*   **Purpose:** I shall use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of completing the defined DevOps project scope.
*   **Protection:** I will take reasonable precautions to protect the Confidential Information, using at least the same degree of care as I use for my own proprietary information.

## 3. Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
*   Is or becomes generally known to the public through no fault of my own.
*   Was rightfully in my possession before the start of this engagement.
*   Is independently developed without the use of your Confidential Information.

## 4. Return or Destruction of Materials
Upon the termination of our contract, or at your written request, I will promptly return or certify the destruction of all documents, files, and credentials containing your Confidential Information.

## 5. Term
The obligations of this Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of our professional engagement and for a period of **two (2) years** following the termination of the project.
